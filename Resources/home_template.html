Frequently Asked Questions

What is GitHub Trending RSS?

GitHub Trending RSS is a service that converts GitHub's trending repositories into RSS feeds, making it easy to stay updated with the most popular projects in your favorite programming languages.

How often are the feeds updated?

The feeds are automatically updated daily to ensure you get the latest trending repositories.

How do I use these RSS feeds?

To use these RSS feeds, follow these simple steps:

  1. Choose your preferred programming language from the sections above
  2. Select a time period (daily, weekly, or monthly)
  3. Click the link to get the RSS feed URL
  4. Copy the URL and paste it into your favorite RSS reader (like Feedly, Follow, Inoreader, or NewsBlur)
  5. Your reader will now automatically fetch updates about trending repositories in your chosen language

Most modern browsers also support RSS feeds directly, allowing you to bookmark and check updates easily.

Why use RSS feeds for GitHub trends?

RSS feeds provide a convenient way to monitor trending repositories without having to visit GitHub regularly. They can be integrated into your daily workflow through RSS readers.
